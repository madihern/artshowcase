Exactly

A riot of pattern and colour compressed into a single word. Each letter becomes its own world — checkered, chevron, swirled, striped — while the background refuses to stay quiet, pushing forward with equal intensity.

About the work

Exactly is a study in controlled chaos. The word itself is assertive — definitive — yet every surface within it is in constant visual motion. No two letters share the same pattern, and no area of the background repeats itself.

The tension between the rigid letterforms and the organic, hand-drawn fills is what gives the piece its energy. It asks the viewer to look closely: the more you look, the more you find.

Created entirely with markers and fine-line pens, the work demonstrates a meticulous hand and an instinct for colour relationships across a wide, saturated spectrum.

Patterns & colours

  • Concentric arcs — burnt orange & green
  • Diamond grid — teal & cobalt
  • Flowing waves — violet & salmon
  • Chevron stripes — lime & forest green
  • Cross-hatch — rust & terracotta
  • Swirls & dots — magenta & purple
